11. Your Privacy Rights

This section provides privacy notices for residents in the European Union and California.

Rights for European Residents :
The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), a regulation governing data privacy in Europe, became effective in May 2018. Individuals in the European Union have the following GDPR rights:

  • Right to be informed
  • Right of access
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to the processing of your personal information
  • Rights around automated decision making and profiling

Rights for California Residents :
The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) is the State’s data privacy regulation, and it went into effect in January 2020. Individuals' entire CCPA rights are as follows:

  • Right to know
  • Right to delete
  • Right to opt-out of sale or sharing
  • Right to non-discrimination
  • Right to correct
  • Right to limit use and disclosure of sensitive personal information

You can exercise the aforementioned rights by contacting us. We may require you to provide relevant information to verify your identity. Any request will be considered and acted upon in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

Please be aware that sharing your personal information to our service providers does not constitute a sale. Your Personal Information will not be sold (as defined by the CCPA) to other parties by AURREUM . Service providers are legally required to use the personal information solely to carry out services for us and not to sell it. AURREUM will guarantee that any service provider with whom we disclose personal data contractually undertakes to protect it and not sell it or otherwise trade it for profit.
